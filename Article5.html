Is my God your God?

By Lebron Subrado  |  February 2026

IIs my God your God? Honestly, I'm not sure that question even needs an answer. What I do know is that I believe, not because someone told me to, not because a religion handed me a rulebook, but because something in me refuses to accept that all of this exists for no reason.

I don't have proof. Nobody does. Science hasn't confirmed it, and philosophy hasn't settled it. But belief was never really about proof to begin with. It's about what you feel when you're at your lowest and something pulls you through. It's about the quiet, unexplainable sense that you are not entirely alone in this.

"God may be unproven, but so is the reason I'm still here."

Religion, to me, is a separate conversation. I respect people who find structure and community through it, but my belief doesn't live inside a building or a specific set of rituals. It lives in the moments where I should have given up and didn't. In the fact that existence itself feels like too deliberate a thing to have happened by accident.

Maybe I'm wrong. I hold that possibility with honesty. But I'd rather live believing in something that gives life weight and meaning than spend my days certain of nothing. One God, no label, no condition — just belief. That's enough for me.
