In Memory of My Past

By Lebron Subrado  |  February 2026

High school didn't just give me memories, it gave me people who quietly shaped who I am. The friends I made during those years taught me things no classroom ever could. Loyalty, honesty, how to laugh through difficult days, and how to show up for someone even when it's inconvenient.

Not every friendship survived the distance that comes after graduation, and that's okay. Some people enter your life to leave something behind, not to stay forever. I've made peace with that.

"High school ended. What they gave me didn't."

Looking back, I don't see a chapter I've finished. I see the foundation of everything I'm still building. Those friendships, however they ended, made me someone worth knowing — and that's not something I take lightly.
