My, my President!

By Lebron Subrado  |  February 2026

From my perspective, observing Philippine politics has made me reflect on the leadership of Ferdinand Marcos Jr. As the current and the 17th president of the Philippines, he represents a political era that carries both modern expectations and historical connections. Being the son of Ferdinand Marcos, his presidency often reminds me how history and present governance can sometimes intersect in complex ways.

I notice that one of the things often discussed about his administration is its focus on economic recovery and national development. I have seen information about programs that support infrastructure projects, agriculture, and digital technology. For me, these efforts seem to reflect an attempt to rebuild and strengthen the country after the economic difficulties brought by global events in recent years.

In my understanding, foreign relations have also been an important part of his leadership. I observe the Philippines continued participation in the Association of Southeast Asian Nations and other international discussions. Issues about the West Philippine Sea remain significant because they are closely connected to national sovereignty and regional cooperation.

“Leadership is not about the name a president carries, but about the lives he helps shape and the future he tries to build for his people.”

At the same time, I have heard different opinions about his administration. Some people express support for his policies, while others raise concerns about historical interpretation, governance decisions, and social issues. These differing views make me realize how leadership is often evaluated through the lens of personal experiences and social perspectives.

Overall, I feel that the presidency of Ferdinand Marcos Jr. is part of the continuing story of the Philippines political journey. As I watch my country face economic and political challenges, I believe that leadership is not judged by a single opinion but by how it affects the lives of ordinary Filipinos over time.
