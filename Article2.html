The Devil That Destroys Me

By Lebron Subrado  |  February 2026

The devil that destroys me is not a creature of darkness, nor a monster hidden in shadows. It is something more silent and more powerful. Time. Unlike visible enemies, time does not attack with force or hatred. Instead, it moves steadily, carrying away moments I wish I could hold forever. I feel its presence in the fading of memories and the quiet realization that life keeps moving whether I am ready or not.

I see time as a devil because it does not wait for my healing, my dreams, or my hesitation. When I want to stay in a moment of happiness, time pulls me forward without mercy. hen I struggle with mistakes, it continues ticking, reminding me that opportunities may not return. It teaches me that some things are irreversible, and some doors close even when I am still standing in front of them.

There are nights when I feel the weight of time more strongly, especially when I think about the paths I have not taken. I wonder about the versions of myself that might have existed if I acted faster or believed in myself earlier. Time does not speak, yet it answers through aging memories, changed relationships, and dreams that slowly lose their brightness if left unattended.

“Better three hours too soon than a minute too late”

However, I also realize that this devil is not only an enemy but also a teacher. Time forces me to grow, to decide, and to value what is present before it disappears. Through its relentless movement, I learn patience, resilience, and acceptance. The same force that takes away moments also gives new chances to rebuild and move forward.

In the end, I understand that time is the devil that destroys me only if I allow fear and regret to dominate my heart. If I live with purpose and awareness, time becomes not an enemy but a companion in my journey. I may never stop time, but I can choose how I live while it continues to walk beside me.
